Mechanism Round — is there interpretable room left in entries/exits?

Part A: descriptive mechanism studies (full history, 10k bootstraps) · Part B: 5 mechanism-motivated rule variants under the same nested walk-forward as the open search
Verdict: no rule-shape room. All five mechanism-motivated changes — depth normalization, 2-bar persistence, buy-the-lag, price-side exits, and capping the extreme percentile tail (post-hoc) — lose to the incumbent rules by 72–130pp of chained OOS return. The mechanism studies explain why: the response to USDT issuance is immediate AND persistent (~2 weeks), so immediate entry + flow-decay exits already match the shape. The remaining interpretable room is in data (exchange-deposit flows, funding absorption, real-time mint feeds), not in rule geometry.
